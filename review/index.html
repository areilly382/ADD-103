The Holdovers

The Holdovers is an emotional and movie story which follows Angus Tully, portraid by Dominic Sessa, a student who has to stay at school over winter break with his teacher Paul Hunham, portraid by Paul Giamatti, and Mary Lamb, portraid by Da'Vine Joy Randolph. During this winter break each character learns to deal with their frustration of being forced to stay, thus forcing them to be alone for christmas. The movie does an amazing job of not only pulling you into the story, but feeling everything with them as well. You watch as each character opens up about their own griefs, regrets, and problems, as they slowly go from hating each other to learning to be compasionate. Helping to prove that no matter who you are, company and community is always a part of healing, emotions may be childish, and joy exists where you create it.

Problamatic Children hate Problamatic Adults

The main problem the characters are encountered with is Tully and Hunham's seeming hatred for each other. Hunham is a strict teacher who punishes much faster than he rewards, and Tully is a student who would faster throw an insult than an apology. both blame each other for their predicament and start of hating each other. In their time together they both learn of the grief they deal with individually. This helps them as part of this grief is Tully feeling as if he has been abandoned by his mother, and Hunahm regreting that he chose work over life. The real kicker is learning about Lamb's own childloss and seeing Tully and Hunham realize how selfish they have been. As well as simultaniously teaching each other how to be stricter, how to relax, and how to love.
